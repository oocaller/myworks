The Starter

Costco was co-founded by James Sinegal in 1983. Known for his customer-first approach and dedication to fair business practices, Sinegal played a key role in turning Costco into one of the largest and most trusted retail chains in the world.

Global Presence

Costco has grown into an international retailer with over 800 warehouse locations in countries such as the United States, Canada, Mexico, the United Kingdom, Japan, South Korea, Australia, and more. Its global success is based on low prices, quality products, and customer loyalty.

Conception and Future of Costco

Costco began with a bold idea: to create a warehouse club that offers quality products at the lowest possible prices. The first location opened in Seattle in 1983, and it quickly became popular for its unique model—offering bulk items at wholesale prices to paying members.

Looking forward, Costco continues to expand globally while focusing on sustainability, efficient supply chains, and improving the member experience. The company is investing in green energy, digital shopping options, and expanding into new markets around the world.
